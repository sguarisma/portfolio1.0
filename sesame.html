Sesame Care


Redesigning Sesame Care's user experience to enhance personalization, accessibility, and clarity.

Role

Context

Sesame Care is a healthcare start-up committed to affordable and high-quality care. As a part of a four-person team, I collaborated on the iterative design of Sesame's developing AI-powered health education platform.

Problem

Patients often struggle to understand medical information, mainly because of the many times confusing, generic, and overwhelming formats it is displayed through. For Sesame, the goal in designing their digital experience was to avoid:

  • Overwhelming and technical medical language
  • Generic content that fails to engage
  • Low-motivation for users to follow through on care or education material

Research

We started by defining our target audience: cost-conscious healthcare consumers looking for clarity, transparency, and autonomy. From meeting with Sesame's CPO (Ravi Mehta) and Lead Product Desiner (Chris Chon), we synthesized our core design goals from their priorities to be:

  • Creating personalized and engaging webpages
  • Provide the user with enhanced understanding and personalized learning
  • Accessibility and clarity, making sure information is easy to understand and the website is easy to navigate.

We kept users with varying health literacy in mind and aimed to simplify interfaces without sacrificing functionality. Our design decisions were also guided by feedback from Vanessa Cho (VP, Google Design Platforms).

We then converged our sketches into a shared wireframe using Balsamiq. Together, we decided to design four core pages: a Welcome Page, Personal Profile Page, Symptoms Page, and a Health Tip page. We took various features from each sketch. For example, we liked the idea of a side information panes like shown in Valentina's sketch, a personalized patient portal page like in my sketch, and the minimalist welcome page like in Kaley and Allen's sketch.

From there, we moved into iterative critique and refinement. After our first critique session with Sesame's team, we received helpful feedback that guided our next versions, including:

  • Using clearer visual hierachy to guide users
  • Eliminate clutter and reduce visual noice
  • Clarify interactive elements, especially on the symptoms page

Solution

We integrated this feedback to create a high-fidelity Figma prototype, which we presented in a final critique session with Sesame. The final prototype is a more engaging, clear educational experience that makes it easier for users to understand their health and take personalized actions. My main contribution was translating our original Balsamiq sketch to Figma and improving clarity and interactions.

Learnings

This project taught me a lot about designing in iterative stages, incorporating feedback from stakeholders, and collaborating with a design team. I learned how to make complex information more actionable, center users with different needs, particularly in a healthcare context, and balance personalization, accessibility, and simplicity in a unified design. This was an incredibly rewarding project to work on and I am thankful I had the opportunity to work with my team and the Sesame Team on this design. In the future, I would love to improve upon our design by making the scale of components more standard and breaking the symtoms page up more into a guided experience for users, as recommended by the Sesame team in our final critique.

I would like to thank Ravi Mehta, Chris Chon, Vanessa Cho, and Caroline Walters for their time and feedback.
